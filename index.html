Stewardship, access, and community, made visible.

What DoD does

Build, maintain, advocate, gather.

From Oakridge and Carpenter Bypass to Thurston Hills, Ridgeline conversations, and future Eugene/Springfield trail systems, DoD's role is to keep mountain biking visible, responsible, and organized.

  • Coordinate volunteer trail work and report hours.
  • Partner with land managers on access and maintenance.
  • Support events that bring riders into the club.
  • Help new riders learn where, when, and how to ride responsibly.
FAQ

Start here.

I'm new to the area. Where can I ride?

Locally, look at Thurston Hills, Eugene Ridgeline shared-use routes, Carpenter Bypass, North Shore, and Oakridge. Conditions matter, especially in winter.

How do I meet more riders?

Come to a Happy Ride, Taco Tuesday at Thurston, a trail work day, or introduce yourself in the Eugene mountain bike community online.

I don't know anything about trail work. Can I help?

Yes. Every volunteer starts somewhere. Experienced trail stewards can teach brushing, raking, tread repair, drainage, hauling, and tool safety.

Why join instead of just following online?

Membership funds trail building, maintenance, advocacy, insurance, tools, and events. It also shows land managers that mountain bikers are organized.

Can donations be tax-deductible?

DoD is a registered nonprofit. Donations may be tax-deductible; check your own tax situation for details.

Why are you called Disciples of Dirt?

Because the club loves dirt, singletrack, and the good work of keeping trails open, sustainable, and fun.
